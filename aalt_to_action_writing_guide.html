1AALT Experience Recap (400 Words)

Let's capture your impressions and learning from the AALT conference. Answer these prompts first—they'll help you write a strong recap to share with your parents.

Prompting Questions

🎯 What was your first impression walking into the program?

What stood out? How did you feel? (nervous, excited, overwhelmed, inspired?)

📋 Tell us about the mock committee process and AB 1644.

What was it like to debate this bill? Did you speak up? What arguments did you find compelling? Which side did you lean toward?

👥 Did you meet any politicians, speakers, or other young leaders?

Who impressed you? What did they say that stuck with you? Any conversations that changed how you think?

🧠 What was the biggest thing you learned?

About government? About yourself? About your power as a young person? About civic engagement?

🔮 What's next for you after AALT?

Are you more interested in politics/law? Will you stay engaged civically? What's your takeaway?

Your Draft Recap (Edit as Needed)

Next step: Copy this text, refine it, make it your own voice. Aim for ~400 words. Share with parents for feedback.

2Choose Your Issue

What issue matters to YOU? This will guide your advocacy letter and your research into representatives' positions.

💭 What policy issue from AALT or from the Navigate & Thrive portfolio matters most to you?

Examples: Education equity, homelessness, voting access, criminal justice, climate, housing, healthcare, civic engagement...

❓ Why does this issue matter to you?

How does it affect you, your family, your community?

💡 What would you WANT to change about this issue?

Be specific. What policy change would help? What would you ask your representative to do?

💭 Thinking about this: Your issue will guide your research into what representatives have said/done about it, and it's the foundation of your advocacy letter.

3Research Your Representatives' Positions

Now that you have an issue, research what YOUR representatives have said or done about it. Fill in what you find below.

🔍 How to Research:

• Search "[Rep Name] [Your Issue]" on Google News
• Check their official website/social media (press releases, statements)
• Look for voting records on state/federal bills related to your issue
• Check local news outlets (LA Times, local news channels)
• Read their positions on their official campaign/government website

Document What You Find

Tip: Repeat this for 2-3 representatives (especially City Council, State Assemblyman, and one or two others). This will inform your advocacy letter.

6Draft, Edit, Deliver

This guide is your workspace. Here's how to turn your drafts into deliverables for your parents.

Step 1: Draft (You, in this guide)

Use this tool to get your ideas down. Don't worry about perfection—just write.

Step 2: Review with Parents

Share your drafts. Get feedback on tone, clarity, impact. Refine.

Step 3: Polish

Fix grammar, strengthen arguments, ensure it sounds like YOU.

Step 4: Finalize & Send/Submit

For thank you letters: Mail or email to representative
For advocacy letter: Send to representative's office
For recap: Share with parents as part of your AALT deliverable

Your AALT Deliverable Package (For Parents)

When complete, you'll have:

  • ✓ 400-word recap of AALT experience
  • ✓ Research on your issue + representative positions
  • ✓ Thank you letter(s) to politicians you met
  • ✓ Advocacy letter on your chosen issue
💡 This demonstrates: Critical thinking, civic engagement, communication skills, and follow-through. Perfect for college applications, resumes, or just showing your parents what you learned.
